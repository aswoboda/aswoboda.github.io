About

Teaching economics as a way of seeing

I am an economist at Carleton College with appointments in Economics and Environmental Studies. My research and teaching focus on how economic incentives shape environmental outcomes — and how careful empirical work can help us understand what policies actually work.

In the classroom, I am drawn to questions that start in the real world: Why do some neighborhoods have worse air quality? What does rent control actually do to housing markets? How much do local schools affect life outcomes? These are not just interesting puzzles — they are the kinds of questions that good empirical economics can help answer, and that answers to can make the world better.
